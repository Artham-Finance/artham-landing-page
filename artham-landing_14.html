The accounting function is broken

Not because of bad software. Because the entire system was designed for compliance first and clarity last. Founders deserve the reverse.

Problem 01

The people problem

Good accountants are hard to find. The ones you do find, leave. Every time they do, the process walks out with them — because there was no process, just a person. Your finances shouldn't depend on whether someone shows up on Monday.

Problem 02

The jargon wall

You ask "how much cash do I have?" and get a trial balance. You ask "who hasn't paid?" and get an accounts receivable ageing report in a format that needs another explanation.

Problem 03

The parallel Excel

Your books are 45 days behind. 15 bills are pending upload. TDS hasn't been deducted. Input credits aren't reconciled. So you give up and open a spreadsheet to track things yourself. Now you have two systems, and neither is right.

Hands-free, real-time accounting

Bills in. Bank statements in. Clarity out. That's it.

01
📸

Snap or forward your bills

Photograph a vendor invoice or forward it to your Artham inbox. We read it — vendor name, amount, GST, TDS, due date — and log it instantly. First bill from a vendor? The party is auto-created. Next bill? Already tagged. Duplicates? Filtered out.

02
🏦

Drop in your bank statement

Upload a PDF or CSV. Artham matches every payment to the right invoice and flags anything unmatched. You see who you've paid, who you haven't, and what's still outstanding — without knowing what "reconciliation" means.

03
📊

See everything, instantly

One screen. Cash in bank. Who owes you. What you owe. What's overdue. Every bill categorized, every vendor tracked, every payment matched. Updated in real time, not 45 days later.

04
🎯

Track against your budget

Set a budget once. As bills come in — photographed, forwarded, or entered — spend updates automatically against each category. No month-end surprises. No "let me check with the accountant."
